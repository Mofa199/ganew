Biography

Professor Michael Thompson is a world-renowned researcher and academic leader in the field of tropical medicine and infectious diseases. With over 25 years of research experience, he has made significant contributions to understanding and combating infectious diseases that affect millions worldwide.

Prof. Thompson earned his Ph.D. in Microbiology from Oxford University and completed post-doctoral research at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). He currently serves as Research Director at the International Institute for Tropical Medicine and holds a joint appointment as Professor of Infectious Diseases at the University of Cambridge.

Academic Positions

  • Research Director, International Institute for Tropical Medicine (2015-Present)
  • Professor of Infectious Diseases, University of Cambridge (2010-Present)
  • Associate Professor, London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine (2005-2010)
  • Senior Research Fellow, Oxford University (2000-2005)

Research Achievements

  • Developed novel diagnostic tools for malaria detection in rural settings
  • Published over 200 peer-reviewed research papers in high-impact journals
  • Led international research collaborations across 25 countries
  • Recipient of the Tropical Medicine Research Excellence Award (2019)
  • Principal Investigator on research grants totaling over $50 million

Current Research Focus

  • Development of rapid diagnostic tests for tropical diseases
  • Antimicrobial resistance in developing countries
  • Vector-borne disease surveillance and control
  • Climate change impact on infectious disease patterns
  • Community-based intervention strategies
