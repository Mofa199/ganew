Biography

Dr. Sarah Johnson is a distinguished medical professional with over two decades of experience in global health initiatives and medical education reform. Currently serving as Chief Medical Officer at the International Health Foundation, she has been instrumental in developing innovative healthcare solutions for underserved communities worldwide.

Dr. Johnson completed her medical degree at Harvard Medical School, followed by a residency in Internal Medicine at Johns Hopkins Hospital. She later pursued a Master's in Public Health from the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, specializing in global health policy and management.

Professional Experience

  • Chief Medical Officer, International Health Foundation (2018-Present)
  • Director of Global Health Programs, World Health Alliance (2012-2018)
  • Senior Medical Advisor, United Nations Health Initiative (2008-2012)
  • Consultant Physician, NHS Trust, London (2005-2008)

Key Achievements

  • Led the implementation of digital health solutions in 15 African countries
  • Authored over 50 peer-reviewed publications on global health and medical education
  • Recipient of the Global Health Innovation Award (2020)
  • Keynote speaker at over 100 international medical conferences
  • Mentor to over 200 medical students and young healthcare professionals

Research Interests

  • Digital health technologies in resource-limited settings
  • Medical education curriculum development for global health
  • Healthcare system strengthening in developing countries
  • Telemedicine and remote healthcare delivery
  • Health policy and healthcare access equity

Conference Presentation

"Transforming Medical Education for Global Health Impact"

Dr. Johnson will present on innovative approaches to medical education that prepare future healthcare professionals for global health challenges. Her presentation will cover curriculum development, practical training methodologies, and the integration of technology in medical education.
