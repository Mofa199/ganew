Biography

Dr. Amina Hassan is a distinguished public health specialist with over 18 years of experience in community health, preventive medicine, and health program management. She has dedicated her career to improving health outcomes in underserved communities across the African continent.

Dr. Hassan earned her medical degree from the University of Nairobi and later completed a Master's in Public Health from the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. She also holds a Doctorate in Health Policy and Management from the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health.

Professional Experience

  • Director of Community Health Programs, African Health Initiative (2018-Present)
  • Senior Public Health Advisor, World Health Organization Africa (2014-2018)
  • Country Health Program Manager, USAID Kenya (2010-2014)
  • District Medical Officer, Ministry of Health Kenya (2006-2010)

Key Contributions

  • Implemented community health worker programs in 12 African countries
  • Led maternal and child health initiatives reaching over 2 million beneficiaries
  • Developed innovative mobile health solutions for rural healthcare delivery
  • Authored 40+ publications on community health and preventive medicine
  • Trained over 5,000 community health workers across East Africa

Areas of Expertise

  • Community-based health program design and implementation
  • Maternal and child health interventions
  • Health systems strengthening in resource-limited settings
  • Digital health and mHealth solutions
  • Health policy development and advocacy
  • Preventive medicine and health promotion strategies

Conference Presentation

"Building Resilient Community Health Systems: Lessons from the Field"

Dr. Hassan will share practical insights from her extensive field experience in building sustainable community health systems. Her presentation will cover successful models, implementation challenges, and strategies for scaling effective interventions.
