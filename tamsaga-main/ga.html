Assembly Overview

The 15th TAMSA General Assembly represents the democratic heart of our organization, bringing together delegates from medical schools across Tanzania and affiliated institutions from the broader East African region. This biennial gathering serves as the supreme decision-making body of TAMSA, where policy directions are set, leadership is elected, and the future of medical education advocacy is determined.

Over two intensive days, delegates will participate in committee sessions, plenary discussions, and electoral processes that will shape TAMSA's direction for the next two years. The assembly combines governance with professional development, featuring capacity building sessions and strategic planning workshops.

Electoral Procedures

Eligibility Criteria

For Candidates:

  • Active TAMSA member for minimum 2 years
  • Good academic standing
  • No disciplinary actions in past 2 years
  • Endorsement from local chapter

For Voters:

  • Registered delegate from member institution
  • Current membership fees paid
  • Attendance at pre-GA orientation
  • Valid student identification

Voting Process

1

Secret Ballot

All voting conducted via secret ballot to ensure privacy and independence

2

Simple Majority

Candidates require 50% + 1 of valid votes cast to win

3

Run-off Elections

If no candidate reaches majority, run-off between top 2 candidates

4

Independent Oversight

Electoral Committee ensures fair and transparent process
